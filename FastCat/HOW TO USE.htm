HOW TO USE THE VOCABULARIES SECTION

Inside this section you will find all terms used in the records that you have published to Fast Cat team. Here you can translate or group your terms under broader terms. In order to do that follow those steps:

1.       Type your template type in the filtering box in the “used in template” column in order to find all vocabularies used in your template type.

2.       The number next to the “Vocabulary title” indicates how many terms are included inside this specific vocabulary.

3.       Choose the first vocabulary (that includes at least one term), go to the gear button and choose “Go to vocabulary”

4.       In order to translate your terms go to the gear button and choose “Edit term”

5.       Add a preferred term, press enter and choose save. Preferred terms should be the English translation of the term. If the preferred term already exists in the list choose the term from the list.

6.       If you believe that the users of the research space would like to search a broader term instead of the specific term existing in your source then add a broader term in the same way next to all terms that you would to group under this broader term. E.g. In the vocabulary “Religions” you could group “Greek-orthodox” and “Catholic” under “Christian” religions.

7.       If you find spelling mistakes that have been made by yourself and were not included in the source, go to the position info to find where this term was used. Then go back to Fast Cat and correct your term in all the positions it was used in your Fast Cat record (You can choose the “Edit vocabulary” functionality. Then save your already “published” record and refresh the Fast Cat team page to see the changes.

8.        If you see mistakes that have been made by the person who created the source then you leave the original term as it is and add the correction into the “Preferred term field”.

Additional information:

·         The position info is dark orange when the term is used in more than one different positions.

·         If one of the records that includes this terms has been published to the Research space the status next to the term will change to “published”.

 

HOW TO USE THE LOCATION SECTION

Inside this section you will find all locations used in the records that you have published to Fast Cat team. Here you can add information for the identity of locations that you would like to appear on maps in the Research space. In order to do that follow those steps:

1.       Choose your template type in the filtering box in the top left corner and choose also the specific record. All locations used in this record will appear.

2.       There are three ways to add the needed identity information for a location:

a.       You can add (type of location)/Broader location (e.g. Barcelona (city)/Spain) next to the Location name that was mentioned in the source inside the Fast Cat record. This is done during the data entry in Fast Cat.
In the case of spelling mistakes, illegible content or non latin alphabet you can replace the source location name with a preferred location name in Fast Cat team.

b.      You can add a TGN id of the Location (provided by the Getty Location database) inside Fast Cat teamà Locations (And choose an preferred term used by the Getty Location database)

c.       You can add coordinates in Fast Cat team. That way the Location will appear on a map in the Research space results but you will not know if the city or the port or the whole region was meant in the source. So better choose this solution just if you don’t have another choice.

3.       If you find spelling mistakes that have been made by yourself and were not included in the source, go to the position info to find where this location was used. Then go back to Fast Cat and correct your location in all the positions it was used in your Fast Cat record (You can choose the “Edit vocabulary” functionality. Then save your already “published” record and refresh the Fast Cat team page to see the changes.

4.       If you see mistakes that have been made by the person who created the source then you leave the original location name as it is and add the correction into the “Preferred location name field”.

Additional information:

·         The position info is dark orange when the location is used in more than one different positions.

·         If one of the records that includes this location has been published to the Research space the status next to the location will change to “published”.

HOW TO USE THE LEGAL ENTITIES SECTION

Inside this section you will find all legal entities used in the records that you have published to Fast Cat team. This is just a list of Legal Entities proposed by the system. The historian will decide whether two or more legal entities that appear in this list are the same or if two legal entities with the same information are different.  In order to do that follow those steps:

1.       Choose your template type in the filtering box in the top left corner and choose also the specific record. All legal entities used in this record will appear.

2.       If you see two legal entities in the list, and you are sure that those two lines refer to the same Legal entity, then go to the check box and choose all Legal Entities you want to merge. Then go to the top left corner and choose “Mark as same”. A pop up window will appear. For each field you can choose a preferred term. If you don’t want to choose one of the existing terms then you can add a new one. When you choose “Save changes” the Legal Entities that have been merged will have only one check box on their left side. The preferred term will appear in bold. If you added a preferred term it will appear in a separate row below.

3.       In order to undo the merging go to the gear button on the left and choose “Undo mark as same”

4.       In order to change the preferred term of the group or of a separate Legal Entity go to the gear button and choose “ Add/Remove preferred term”.

5.       If you go to the position info of an Legal Entity and you see that two appearances in different positions refer to another Legal Entity, then press to the button “ Mark as different” on the right side.  The two Legal Entities will appear in separate rows in the list.

Additional information:

·         The position info is dark orange when the Legal Entity is used in more than one different positions.

·         If one of the records that includes this Legal Entity has been published to the Research space the status next to the Legal Entity will change to “published”.

·         Inside this list all Legal Entities of the same template type that have identical identity information appear in a separate row.
