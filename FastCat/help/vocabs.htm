HOW TO USE THE VOCABULARIES SECTION

Inside this section you will find all terms used in the records that you have published to Fast Cat team.

Main vocabulary page:
In this page you will find a list with all vocabularies that exist in all template types.

The name of the vocabulary is the name of the category of terms and sometimes might not coincide with the name of the related field of your template (e.g. in Census la Ciotat exists a filed “Role in household”, this vocabulary can be found in the “Person to person relation” Vocabulary).

This page is static which means that the related templates are always there either this vocabulary is filled in with terms or not.

Only the number of terms is dynamically renewed (the counting of terms is approximate).

How to process your terms:

Inside the specific vocabulary page you can translate or group your terms under broader terms.

 In order to do that follow those steps:

Main Vocabulary page: 

1.       Type your template type in the filtering box in the “used in template” column in order to find all vocabularies used in your template type.

2.        The number next to the “Vocabulary title” indicates how many terms are included inside this specific vocabulary.

3.       Choose the first vocabulary (that includes at least one term), go to the gear button and choose “Go to vocabulary”

 

Specific Vocabulary page:  

 

4.       In order to translate your terms go to the gear button and choose “Edit term”

5.       Add a preferred term, press enter and choose save. Preferred terms should be the English translation of the term. If the preferred term already exists in the list choose the term from the list.

6.      If you believe that the users of the research space would like to search a broader term instead of the specific term existing in your source then add a broader term in the same way next to all terms that you would to group under this broader term. E.g. In the vocabulary “Religions” you could group “Greek-orthodox” and “Catholic” under “Christian” religions.

7.     If you find spelling mistakes that have been made by yourself and were not included in the source, go to the position info to find where this term was used. Then go back to Fast Cat and correct your term in all the positions it was used in your Fast Cat record (You can choose the “Edit vocabulary” functionality. Then save your already “published” record and refresh the Fast Cat team page to see the changes.

8.       If you see mistakes that have been made by the person who created the source then you leave the original term as it is and add the correction into the “Preferred term field”.

Additional information:

·         The position info is dark orange when the term is used in more than one different positions.

·         If one of the records that includes this terms has been published to the Research space the status next to the term will change to “published”
